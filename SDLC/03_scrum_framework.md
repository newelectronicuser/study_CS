# Scrum Framework

Scrum is the most popular Agile framework for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products.

## 1. Scrum Roles
- **Product Owner (PO)**: Responsible for maximizing the value of the product. Owns the Product Backlog and defines "What" needs to be built.
- **Scrum Master (SM)**: Facilitates the Scrum process and helps the team remove impediments (blockers). Not a manager, but a servant-leader.
- **Development Team**: A cross-functional, self-organizing group of people who do the work (developers, designers, testers).

## 2. Scrum Ceremonies (Events)
- **The Sprint**: A fixed-length event of one month or less to create a "Done" increment.
- **Sprint Planning**: Gathering at the start of a sprint to define what can be delivered and how the work will be achieved.
- **Daily Stand-up**: A 15-minute time-boxed event for the team to sync and plan for the next 24 hours.
- **Sprint Review**: Held at the end of the sprint to inspect the increment and adapt the Product Backlog. (Demo to stakeholders).
- **Sprint Retrospective**: Held after the Review to inspect how the last sprint went and identify improvements for the next one.

## 3. Scrum Artifacts
- **Product Backlog**: An ordered list of everything that might be needed in the product.
- **Sprint Backlog**: A set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint, plus a plan for delivering the Increment.
- **Increment**: The sum of all the Product Backlog items completed during a Sprint and the value of the increments of all previous Sprints.

## 4. Definitions
- **Definition of Done (DoD)**: A shared understanding of what it means for a piece of work to be "completed" (e.g., Code reviewed, tested, documented).
- **Sprint Goal**: An objective that will be met within the Sprint.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> A common interview question: "What is the difference between a Product Backlog and a Sprint Backlog?"
> - **Product Backlog** is the long-term "wishlist" for the whole project.
> - **Sprint Backlog** is the short-term commitment for the current 1-4 week cycle.

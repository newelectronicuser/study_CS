# Waterfall vs. Agile

Choosing the right methodology depends on the project scope, requirements, and timeline.

## 1. Waterfall Model
A linear, sequential approach where each phase must be completed before the next one begins.
- **Pros**: Easy to manage (fixed scope/budget), clear milestones, disciplined approach.
- **Cons**: High risk (errors found late), inflexible to change, no working software until the end.
- **Best For**: Small projects with clearly defined, stable requirements (e.g., government contracts, physical infrastructure).

## 2. Agile Methodology
An iterative and incremental approach that focuses on continuous feedback and rapid delivery.
- **Agile Manifesto**:
    - **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools.
    - **Working software** over comprehensive documentation.
    - **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation.
    - **Responding to change** over following a plan.
- **Pros**: Flexible, high customer satisfaction, early delivery, reduced risk.
- **Cons**: Difficult to predict final cost/timeline, requires active stakeholder involvement, "scope creep".
- **Best For**: Projects with evolving requirements, complex systems, startups.

## 3. Comparison Table

| Feature | Waterfall | Agile |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Requirements** | Fixed upfront | Evolving/Dynamic |
| **Phase Delivery** | One big release at the end | Incremental releases (Sprints) |
| **Flexibility** | Rigid | Highly Flexible |
| **Customer Involvement** | Mainly at start and end | Continuous throughout |
| **Testing** | After development is over | Concurrent with development |

## 4. Other Models (Briefly)
- **V-Model (Verification and Validation)**: An extension of Waterfall where testing is planned in parallel with the corresponding development phase.
- **Spiral Model**: Combines iterative development with systematic risk assessment.

> [!TIP]
> In an interview, if asked "Which is better?", the best answer is: "It depends on the project's complexity and certainty of requirements. Waterfall is better for low-uncertainty projects, while Agile is better for high-uncertainty projects."
